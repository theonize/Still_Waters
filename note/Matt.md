There is a prepronderence of responsibility and a lack of relationship in the Church.

The circumstances that produce humility are, by design, discouraging;
those that produce longsuffering are necessarily disorienting.

Warmth and sincerity do not assuage a lack of preparation, vision or discernment.

Expectations are often askew in the Church.

Themes:
* Usefulness vs belonging
* Poor communication often masks lack of relationship
* Grace under pressure vs growth of bitterness
* Sincere hearts still fail without premises
* Entrenched habits engineer decline
* Faithfulness vs self-preservation
* Moral complexity vs tradition
* The modern christian experience is anesthetic
